## PART 3: EXPORT CONTROL EVASION MECHANISMS

### Common Tactics Documented
- **Shell Companies:** Front entities in permissive jurisdictions *(Medium detection difficulty)*
- **Dual-Use Classification:** Frame as "commercial software" not surveillance *(High detection difficulty)*
- **End-User Certificates:** Fake documentation on final destination *(Medium detection difficulty)*
- **Third-Party Resellers:** Multiple intermediaries obscure chain *(High detection difficulty)*
- **Cloud-Based Delivery:** Software hosted remotely, accessed by license *(High detection difficulty)*

## PART 6: INTERNATIONAL REGULATION LANDSCAPE

### Regional Approaches
- **EU:** GDPR + AI Act + Digital Services Act *(Moderate enforcement - €20B+ fines issued)*
- **U.S.:** Sectoral (no federal privacy law) *(Low enforcement - state-level patchwork)*
- **China:** Cybersecurity Law + Social Credit *(High enforcement - mandatory compliance)*
- **Canada:** PIPEDA + Bill C-27 proposed *(Low enforcement - weak penalties)*
- **Brazil:** LGPD (similar to GDPR) *(Moderate enforcement)*
- **India:** Personal Data Protection Bill pending *(Low enforcement - implementation delayed)*

### Notable Restrictions & Workarounds
- **Facial recognition bans (EU):** 17 cities + 3 countries limit usage; vendors adapt by shifting to "verification" vs. identification.
- **Predictive policing restrictions:** 12 U.S. states; vendors reclassify systems as "risk assessment tools".
- **Spyware export limits:** ITAR/EAR controls; vendors bypass via dual-use classification.
- **Data localization laws:** 40+ countries; vendors replicate cloud infrastructure locally.
