## Part 2: Fertility Patterns — Do Immigrants Solve Population Decline?

### Multi-Generation Convergence Data
| Generation | Fertility vs. Native-Born | Evidence |
|------------|---------------------------|----------|
| **1st gen (foreign-born immigrants)** | Higher (varies by origin country) | Blau 1991 (NBER), CIS 2017 |
| **2nd gen (children born in host country)**| Converges to native rate | Estévez 2026 (European Sociological Review), Grytten 2024 |
| **3rd gen (grandchildren)** | Often at or below native rate | Baek et al. UK study 2022 |

*Key finding:* Within 1-2 generations, immigrant fertility patterns match host country norms regardless of origin country fertility rates.

### Quantitative Impact on National Fertility
| Metric | Finding | Source |
|--------|---------|--------|
| **US immigrant birth rate decline (2008-2017)**| Fell from 76 to 62 per 1000 (-14 pts) | CIS 2017 |
| **Immigrant fertility's share of national TFR**| Small (<5% impact even if doubled) | CIS 2017 |
| **Canada foreign-born population by 2041** | Projected 34% of total population | Migration Policy Institute |
| **EU average fertility gap (migrant vs. native)**| Ranged from 2% (Netherlands) to 73% (France), 2009-2015 | Bagavos 2019 (NIUSSP) |

*Conclusion:* Immigration provides temporary fertility boost but does not permanently alter trajectory—convergence occurs within two generations.

## Part 7: Pro-Natalist Alternatives — What Works?

### Comparative Policy Effectiveness
| Country | Policy Type | TFR Change | Timeframe |
|---------|-------------|------------|-----------|
| **Poland** | Universal cash benefit (Family 500+) | 1.30 → 1.36 | 2010s-2026 |
| **Hungary** | Targeted loans/subsidies | 1.23 → 1.61 (2020 peak) → 1.39 (2024) | 2010-2026 |
| **Sweden** | Comprehensive childcare + parental leave | ~1.84-1.85 | Sustained |
| **Norway** | Similar Nordic model | ~1.73-1.74 | Sustained |
| **Finland** | Nordic model | ~1.38 | 2025-26 |

*Source:* AEI 2026, IFS Studies 2026, Eurostat 2024
*Finding:* Cash benefits alone have modest, temporary effects. Comprehensive support systems (childcare, parental leave, housing) sustain higher rates but at significant cost.
