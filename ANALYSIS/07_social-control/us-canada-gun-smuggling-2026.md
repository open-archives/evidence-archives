## Executive Summary

While Canada has pursued aggressive domestic gun control policies, including bans on assault-style firearms, the vast majority of crime guns recovered in Canada originate in the United States. Data from the Canada Border Services Agency (CBSA) and provincial police forces indicate that approximately 90% of seized firearms are traced back to U.S. sources, predominantly states with loose firearm regulations such as Ohio, Texas, and Florida.

Latin American cartels play an indirect role, primarily supplying and financing U.S. distribution networks rather than smuggling directly into Canada. Meanwhile, the proliferation of "ghost guns" (untraceable, unassembled firearm parts) has created a growing challenge for border enforcement. This dynamic highlights a core policy contradiction: domestic gun bans in Canada are fundamentally limited by the porous border and the high volume of firearms flowing north from the U.S. black market.

## Part 2: U.S. as the Primary Source

Data consistently shows the United States is the origin point for the vast majority of crime guns in Canada.

| Data Point | Source |
|------------|--------|
| **90%** of CBSA-seized firearms originated in the U.S. | CBSA data |
| **91%** of crime handguns traced in Ontario came from the U.S. | Ontario police |
| **88%** of CBSA seizures (2025) came from the U.S. | CBSA (650 of 740) |
| **21,000+** crime guns traced to U.S. sources | ATF / Canadian authorities |
| **77%** of traced crime guns in London, ON (2025) came from U.S. | London police |

| State | Why It is a Source |

## Part 3: Smuggling Routes & Methods

| Method | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Vehicle concealment** | Guns hidden in trunks, gas tanks, custom compartments |
| **Mail / parcels** | Parts shipped separately; "ghost guns" assembled later |
| **Drone delivery** | Small drones fly across border with guns |
| **Water routes** | Great Lakes, small boats crossing at night |
| **Commercial trucking** | Hidden inside legitimate shipments |

## Part 4: "Ghost Guns" — The Growing Problem

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **What they are** | Untraceable firearms — no serial numbers; sold as kits or 3D-printed |
| **Growing concern** | Increasingly found in Canadian crime guns |
| **How they are made** | Parts sourced from U.S. (online), assembled in Canada |
| **Border risk** | Harder to detect because they cross as parts, not completed guns |

---

## Part 5: Latin American Cartels — The Connection

Cartels are involved in the financial side more than the logistical side of cross-border gun smuggling into Canada.

| Factor | Explanation |
|--------|-------------|
| **Direct link** | Limited. Cartels rarely smuggle directly into Canada. |
| **Indirect link** | Cartels supply U.S. gangs, who then supply Canadian groups. |
| **Straw purchasers** | Cartels use U.S. proxies to buy guns legally, then smuggle north. |
| **Money laundering** | Cartels use Canadian real estate, casinos, and businesses to launder money — gun profits are integrated into this network. |
