## Executive Summary

The United States continues to lead the world in both civilian gun ownership and gun-related deaths. As of July 2026, over 5,000 people have died from gunfire this year alone. While shooting deaths are at their lowest level in over a decade, the scale of gun violence remains staggering, with more than 240 mass shootings recorded so far in 2026.

The gun control debate has shifted significantly: the Trump administration is aggressively challenging state-level restrictions in court, while the Supreme Court has agreed to hear landmark cases on assault weapon bans. The result is a fragmented, state-by-state patchwork of laws with no federal consensus.

## Part 2: Legislative & Legal Landscape (2026)

### Federal Level

The Trump administration has made challenging state gun laws a priority. Acting Attorney General Todd Blanche has threatened lawsuits against states with restrictions similar to those struck down by the Supreme Court.

**Key federal actions:**

| Action | Status | Details |
|--------|--------|---------|
| **Suit against Virginia** | Filed July 2026 | Challenges ban on semiautomatic assault rifles |
| **Suit against California** | Filed July 2026 | Challenges ban on Glock-style pistols |
| **Suit against Denver** | Filed May 2026 | Challenges ban on semiautomatic rifles |
| **ATF regulatory actions** | 34 published May 2026 | Modernizing firearms regulations |

### Supreme Court

| Case | Status | Significance |
|------|--------|--------------|

| **Hawaii carry restrictions** | Struck down June 2026 | 6-3 ruling; affected 4 other states |

## Part 3: The Federal vs. State Divide

| Element | Democratic States | Republican States |
|---------|-------------------|-------------------|
| **Assault weapon bans** | Common (CA, NY, IL, CT, etc.) | Rare or non-existent |
| **Permit requirements** | Strict | Lax or none |
| **Gun sales trend** | Declining (restrictions) | Stable or rising |
| **Legal challenges** | Defending laws | Challenging restrictions |

**The border problem:** East St. Louis, Illinois (strict gun laws) sits on the border with Missouri (no permits, no licensing). Guns flow easily across state lines.
