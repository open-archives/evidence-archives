## Executive Summary

As of July 2026, Canada's approach to gun control involves federal firearm prohibitions and compensation, alongside significant bail and sentencing reforms through Bill C-14. Provinces are adding their own measures, like British Columbia's Firearm Violence Prevention Act. The full impact of these reforms won't be known for 1–2 years.

---

## Part 1: Federal Firearm Control Initiatives

| Initiative | Status | Details |
|------------|--------|---------|
| **Assault-style firearm ban** | Active (since May 2020) | Over 2,500 makes/models prohibited |
| **Assault-Style Firearms Compensation Program (ASFCP)** | Active | Owners can receive compensation for prohibited firearms |
| **Amnesty orders** | Extended | Protects owners from criminal liability while complying (set to expire 90 days after Supreme Court decision on 2020 prohibition) |
| **"Yellow flag" laws** | Implemented March 2025 | Temporary license suspension |
| **License revocation/refusal** | Implemented April 2025 | Enhanced authorities |

---

## Part 2: Bill C-14 — Bail and Sentencing Reform Act

**Effective:** July 15, 2026  
**Scope:** Over 80 amendments to the Criminal Code

### Key Provisions

| Provision | Impact |
|-----------|--------|
| **Reverse onus bail** | Accused must prove why they should be released (for designated offenses) |
| **Consecutive sentencing** | Courts can impose consecutive sentences for violent auto theft, home invasion, arson, extortion |
| **Restricted conditional sentences** | Limited use of house arrest for sexual assault and sex crimes against minors |
| **Firearm prohibitions** | Courts required to consider firearm prohibitions in more cases |

### Designated Offenses (Reverse Onus)

| Offense Category | Reverse Onus Applied? |
|------------------|----------------------|
| Violent auto theft | ✅ |
| Extortion involving violence | ✅ |
| Home invasion | ✅ |
| Human trafficking | ✅ |
| Auto theft for organized crime | ✅ |
| Third violent offense | ✅ |
| Choking/strangulation/suffocation | ✅ |
| Prior similar conviction + firearm | ✅ |

## Part 3: Provincial Actions

### British Columbia — Firearm Violence Prevention Act

| Detail | Information |
|--------|-------------|
| **Effective** | October 1, 2026 |
| **Purpose** | Fill gaps in federal law |
| **Key provisions** | Regulates imitation firearms; restricts presence near schools, hospitals, places of worship; cracks down on discharging firearms from vehicles |

## Part 5: The Debate

### Support

| Group | Position |
|-------|----------|
| **Federal government** | Bans reduce gun violence; C-14 improves public safety |
| **Canadian Police Association** | Welcomed reforms |
| **Mayors (Brampton, Winnipeg)** | Pushed for action |
| **Victims' advocates** | Focuses on public safety |

### Opposition / Concerns

| Group | Concern |
|-------|---------|
| **Firearms owners / advocates** | Bans = confiscation; compensation inadequate |
| **Canadian Civil Liberties Association** | C-14 will hit Indigenous, racialized, low-income communities hardest |
| **Nunavut lawyers** | "Bill C-14 will put more Inuit in jail" |
| **Criminologists** | Won't reduce gun crime; smugglers and gangs remain the issue |
| **Senator Kim Pate** | Will "perpetuate intergenerational trauma" |
