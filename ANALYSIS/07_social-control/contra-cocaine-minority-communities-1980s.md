## Part 2: The Contra Rebellion

### Background
| Fact | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **Opposition Group** | Contra (Nicaraguan Democratic Forces) |
| **US Support Start** | 1981 (Reagan Administration) |
| **Funding Method** | Initially congressional, later private/illegal |
| **Operation** | Covert military action against Sandinista government |
| **Controversy** | Boland Amendment banned direct funding (1984) |

### Congressional Restrictions
| Legislation | Year | Effect |
|-------------|------|--------|
| **Boland Amendment I** | 1982 | Limited CIA/Dept of Defense aid to Contras |
| **Boland Amendment II** | 1984 | Banned direct US government funding |
| **Result** | 1985-1986 | Funding shifted to private donors, potentially illicit sources |

## Part 3: Senate Kerry Committee Investigation (1986)

### Key Finding
> **"The Contra drug links included... payments to drug traffickers by the U.S. State Department of funds authorized by the Congress for humanitarian assistance to the Contras, in some cases after the traffickers had been indicted by federal law enforcement agencies on drug charges, in others while traffickers were under active investigation by these same agencies."**  
> — Senate Foreign Relations Committee Sub-committee (Kerry Committee, 1986)

### What This Means
| Action | Evidence |
|--------|----------|
| **Funds sent to traffickers** | State Department disbursed humanitarian aid |
| **Timing** | Some payments AFTER indictment, some DURING investigation |
| **Agency involved** | U.S. State Department (not CIA directly) |
| **Knowledge level** | Government knew of trafficking allegations |

### Individuals Named
| Person | Allegation | Outcome |
|--------|------------|---------|
| **Adolfo Calero** | Contra leader, received funds | Continued receiving aid despite allegations |
| **Edén Pastora** | Southern Front commander | Trafficking links documented |
| **Various traffickers** | Multiple individuals | Some indicted, some continued receiving funds |

## Part 5: DOJ OIG Investigation (1997)

### Scope
| Component | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Office** | Department of Justice Office of Inspector General |
| **Title** | "CIA-Contra-Crack Cocaine Controversy: A Review of the Justice Department's Investigations" |
| **Release** | December 1997 |
| **Pages** | Over 300-page report |

### Conclusions
| Finding | Status |
|---------|--------|
| **CIA directed drug trafficking** | ❌ No evidence found |
| **CIA encouraged drug trafficking** | ❌ No evidence found |
| **CIA participated in drug trafficking** | ❌ No evidence found |
| **State Dept paid traffickers** | ✅ Confirmed (matches Kerry Committee) |
| **Government failed to act on trafficking** | ✅ Acknowledged |
| **Trafficking contributed to crack epidemic** | ✅ Indirect connection noted |

### Critical Quote
> **"The reports did note that the U.S. government failed to fully investigate or act on known drug-related activities that indirectly contributed to the devastation of inner-city minority communities during that era."**  
> — DOJ OIG (1997)
