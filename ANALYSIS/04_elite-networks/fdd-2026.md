## Executive Summary

The Foundation for Defense of Democracies (FDD) is a Washington, D.C.-based think tank that has positioned itself as a nonpartisan analyst of national security issues. Founded in 2001 by Clifford May, the organization has become a key ideological engine for shaping U.S. Middle East policy, particularly regarding Iran. Critics argue that FDD "functions less as an independent think tank and more as an ideological extension of Israeli security doctrine." Its network includes former Israeli military and intelligence officials who have spent years pushing the U.S. toward confrontation with Iran. With an annual budget of approximately $10 million and access to senior U.S. policymakers, FDD exerts outsized influence relative to its size.

### FDD's Official Position on the Revolving Door

FDD maintains that its employees adhere to a strict "revolving door policy" that prioritizes national security over personal gain. The organization does not directly name specific officials but has publicly stated its commitment to "prevent its experts from using their positions at the organization as a stepping stone for private sector or government roles."
