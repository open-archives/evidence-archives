### Political Donation Rules (2025-2026)
| Element | Rule | Enforcement |
|---------|------|-------------|
| **Individual contribution cap** | $1,750/year (2025), $1,775/year (2026) | Elections Canada enforcement |
| **Corporate donations** | Cannot give directly to parties/candidates | Illegal |
| **Trade/professional association donations** | Allowed through individuals, same $1,750 cap | Regulated |
| **Loans/loan guarantees** | Counted toward same limits | Regulated |
| **Annual increases** | +$25 each January | Automatic |

*Critical Loophole:* While corporations can't donate directly, wealthy individuals (executives, board members) can max out donations to every party simultaneously:
- Liberal Party: $1,775
- Conservative Party: $1,775
- NDP: $1,775
- Bloc Québécois: $1,775
- Leadership contests: $1,775 each
- Registered associations in multiple ridings: $1,775 each

*Result:* A family with 10-20 executives/board members can spread hundreds of thousands across all parties while technically complying with individual caps.

## Part 10: Structural Vulnerabilities

### How Influence Flows
```
Wealth Concentration (top 0.5% = 20% wealth)
         ↓
Elite Family Control (Thomson, Desmarais, Gilgan, etc.)
         ↓
Multiple Channels:
  → Media ownership (Thomsons)
  → Political donations (via executives across parties)
  → Lobbying industry (business-dominated)
  → Revolving door (minister → lobbyist)
  → Elite networks (Canadian Club, Economic Club, B7)
         ↓
Policy Outcomes Favor Elite Interests
         ↓
Wealth Concentration Increases (cycle repeats)
```

### Institutional Weak Points
- **Individual donation caps too low for meaningful restriction:** $1,775 limit per person doesn't stop families with 20+ executives from spreading influence across all parties.
- **Lobbying registry incomplete:** Only captures registered lobbyists, not informal advising, advisory committees, or "consultants" who don't register.
- **Revolving door enforcement weak:** 1-2 year cooling-off periods are short relative to career spans; former officials can work for firms they previously regulated.
- **Media ownership concentration unchecked:** Thomson family controls Globe and Mail without public interest oversight mechanisms.
- **Advisory bodies lack transparency:** Canadian Club, Economic Club, B7 operate as invitation-only forums with no public disclosure of discussions.
