What Corporations Buy with Davos Participation:
- Direct conversations with heads of state (presidents, prime ministers)
- Private dinners with central bank governors
- Access to treasury/finance ministry officials
- Networking with peer corporations for deals/joint ventures
- Brand positioning as "global responsibility leader"
- Ability to shape policy language before it becomes legislation

## Part 5: Transparency Assessment

### What WEF Publishes
- ✅ Partner list (names of Global Strategic, Industry, Regional partners)
- ✅ Session agendas (published before Davos meeting)
- ✅ Publications (Global Risks Report, industry white papers)
- ✅ Leadership bios (board member backgrounds)
- ✅ Open Forum public sessions (free, registered attendance)
- ✅ Centre research outputs (public reports)

### What WEF Does NOT Disclose
- ❌ Private meeting transcripts or notes
- ❌ Bilateral discussion records (government + corporate)
- ❌ Voting records on policy positions (WEF doesn't formally vote)
- ❌ Detailed financial statements (annual revenue breakdown not itemized)
- ❌ Donor names beyond corporate partner tier list
- ❌ Decision-making process for policy priorities

### Tax Status & Financial Disclosure
- WEF = Swiss foundation (nonprofit)
- Receives 30% public funding from Swiss government
- 70% corporate membership fees
- No requirement to disclose detailed donor lists like U.S. 501(c)(3) organizations
