## Part 6: Transparency Assessment

### What CFR Publishes
- ✅ Annual reports with revenue/expenses
- ✅ Corporate member roster (public)
- ✅ Event calendars (mostly)
- ✅ Task force reports (bipartisan)
- ✅ Senior fellowship bios (employment history)
- ✅ Foreign Affairs journal articles

### What CFR Does NOT Disclose
- ❌ Closed-session attendee lists
- ❌ Discussion notes from private events
- ❌ Specific corporate member engagement minutes
- ❌ Donor names for Annual Fund (tax-deductible gifts anonymous)
- ❌ Internal decision-making on policy priorities

### 501(c)(3) Filing Requirements
CFR files Form 990 which shows:
- Top officer compensation
- Revenue breakdown by source
- Largest contributions (over $5,000)
- Program expenses by category

*However: Individual donor names not required for tax-deductible contributions.*

## Part 7: Conflict of Interest Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Defense Contractor → CFR Event → State Department
**Flow:**
1. Lockheed Martin pays Gold-tier CFR membership (~$100k/year)
2. Lockheed exec attends CFR roundtable with current State Department officials
3. Discussions about "Ukraine security framework" and "defense spending priorities"
4. State Department incorporates similar language into foreign aid package
5. Lockheed wins contract to supply equipment
*Potential Conflict: Direct access without public record of discussions*

### Scenario 2: Financial Firm → Treasury → CFR Board
**Flow:**
1. Goldman Sachs hires former Treasury Secretary Paulson
2. Paulson joins CFR board
3. CFR hosts event on "financial stability" with Goldman presence
4. Policy recommendations reflect Goldman expertise/priorities
5. Treasury adopts similar frameworks (when different admin)
*Potential Conflict: Personal financial incentives through post-government employment*

### Scenario 3: Energy Company → CFR Fellows → Energy Policy
**Flow:**
1. ExxonMobil Gold member
2. CFR task force on "energy transition" includes energy sector experts
3. Recommendations emphasize gradual transition, continued oil/gas infrastructure
4. Federal energy policy reflects similar timeline
5. ExxonMobil profits extend
*Potential Conflict: Corporate interests embedded in expert recommendations*
