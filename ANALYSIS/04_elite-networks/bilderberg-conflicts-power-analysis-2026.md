## Part 1: Structure & Governance

### Foundation & Purpose
Founded in 1954 at the Hotel De Bilderberg in Oosterbeek, Netherlands, by Polish politician-in-exile Józef Retinger and others concerned about anti-Americanism in Western Europe. Original purpose: foster Atlanticism—better understanding between U.S. and Western European cultures on political, economic, and defense issues.

### How It Works
| Element | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Frequency** | Annual, 3-4 day meeting |
| **Size** | ~128-130 participants (72nd meeting, 2026) |
| **Composition** | ~⅔ European, ~⅓ North American; ~⅓ government/politics, ~⅔ other fields |
| **Format** | Off-the-record discussions under Chatham House Rule |
| **Outputs** | No minutes, no resolutions, no votes, no reports |
| **Media access** | None—no journalist accreditation |
| **Participant turnover** | ~⅔ change year to year; steering committee provides continuity |
| **Legal entity** | Foundation Bilderberg Meetings (Netherlands) + American Friends of Bilderberg Inc. (US 501(c)(3)) |

### The Chatham House Rule (Since 1927)
Originated at the Royal Institute of International Affairs (Chatham House, London). Obliges all attendees to observe absolute confidentiality regarding conversation content and speakers' statements. Anyone disclosing what was said can be excluded from future meetings.
*Critical implication:* No public record exists of what is discussed, who argued what position, or what agreements (informal or otherwise) may have been reached.
