## Executive Summary

On **February 28, 2026**, the United States and Israel launched joint military strikes against Iran, killing Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei and other senior officials. The war that followed has killed thousands, upended regional security, and disrupted global trade.

The Wesley Clark memo — the 2001 Pentagon document listing seven countries to be "taken out" — had Iran as the final target. Twenty-five years after the memo was written, all seven countries have been engaged.

## Part 7: Iran's Strategy — Asymmetric Warfare

Iran's military response has been limited by its inability to match U.S. and Israeli forces. Instead:

- **Economic warfare** — controlling the Strait, targeting shipping
- **Proxy warfare** — Hezbollah, Houthis, Iraqi militias
- **Asymmetric strikes** — missiles and drones at U.S. bases
- **Diplomatic maneuvering** — using Qatar, Pakistan, Oman as mediators

**Iran's goal:** Force the U.S. to the negotiating table by making the war economically unsustainable.
